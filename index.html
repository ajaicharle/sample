Actor Dr. V. T. M. Charle

Dr. V. T. M. Charle, born T. Manohar, is an Indian actor, theatre artist, researcher, orator, and academician. His work spans Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada, and Hindi cinema.

The Legacy

A career shaped by performance, learning, humour, and public service.

Born T. Manohar in Virudhunagar, Tamil Nadu, to schoolteachers V. Thangasamy and D. Kanagapushpamani, Charle was drawn early to school plays, cultural activities, and theatre.

Introduced to cinema by legendary filmmaker K. Balachander in Poikkal Kudhirai, he received the screen name Charle and went on to become one of South Indian cinema's respected supporting actors.

Theatre

The stage gave him rhythm, reach, and a public purpose.

From 1980 to 1986, Charle served as a Staff Artist in the Song and Drama Division of the Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, Government of India, Southern Region. He performed across India in multilingual productions promoting social awareness, national integration, communal harmony, education, patriotism, and public welfare.
