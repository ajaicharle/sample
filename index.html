Actor Dr. V. T. M. Charle

Dr. V. T. M. Charle, born T. Manohar, is an Indian actor, theatre artist, researcher, orator, and academician primarily associated with Tamil cinema, with work across Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada, and Hindi cinema.

The Legacy

A career built across theatre, cinema, research, public speaking, and social commitment.

Born T. Manohar in Virudhunagar, Tamil Nadu, to schoolteachers V. Thangasamy and D. Kanagapushpamani, Charle was drawn early to school plays, cultural activities, and intercollegiate theatre. His artistic identity was later shaped by discipline, education, and a lifelong commitment to socially conscious performance.

Introduced to cinema by legendary filmmaker K. Balachander in Poikkal Kudhirai, he received the screen name Charle and went on to become one of the most respected supporting actors in South Indian cinema.

Theatre

The stage gave him rhythm, reach, and a public purpose.

From 1980 to 1986, Charle served as a Staff Artist in the Song and Drama Division of the Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, Government of India, Southern Region. He performed across India in multilingual productions promoting social awareness, national integration, communal harmony, education, patriotism, and public welfare.

Research & Public Life

Comedy, culture, and social responsibility studied with seriousness.

His M.Phil. research examined the contribution of comedians to Tamil cinema between 1937 and 1967, while his Ph.D. focused on humour in Tamil cinema. As an orator and cultural ambassador, he has spoken on youth empowerment, gender equality, drug abuse prevention, suicide prevention, cultural values, and social responsibility.
